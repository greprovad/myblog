Instilling Problem-Solving Skills In Kids: Elementary School Project

Posted June 05, 2018, slightly revised June 7, 2018    Kids Education Problem-Solving Skills

Last week I read a fantastic post written by Kyle Racki titled Why Kids Should Be Taught Entrepreneurship Early On. I wanted to stand up in front of my computer and travel back in time to be taught this stuff when I was younger. This post steers me in the right direction in not only thinking-outside-the-box but also in redefining the box by considering positive points that can propel children's efforts when they start resolving conundrums as early as possible.

I currently work on a project named Rakugaku (enjoy studying) as part of social contribution activities initiated by Hitachi Solutions East Japan, Ltd. and Sendai City Education Committee (仙台市教育委員会).

This project aims to introduce programming education in elementary schools by 2020. The vision behind this project is to instill in children programming skills in early age, develop designated skills and prepare them to be innovative and creative, demystify what may later become a brain teaser to them and help them activate an entrepreneurial mindset. This won't be achieved straight away as long as children still have a weak background in problem-solving. Hence, we must start from this fundamental point before introducing to them any software that may make things simpler.

Having said that, I borrowed the point that Kyle Racki has outlined as recommendations proposed by Amy Morin to teach children problem-solving skills:

- Identifying the problem

- Thinking about feasible solutions

- Describing the advantages and disadvantages of each approach

- Selecting the most practical solution

- Testing it out
